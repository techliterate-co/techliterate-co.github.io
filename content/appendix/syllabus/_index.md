### **Course Description**

This course provides a guided introduction to the key components of today’s
technology landscape—from industry roles and hardware to networking,
cybersecurity, and cloud computing. Students will gain both conceptual
understanding and practical skills that apply across all areas of technology.
Whether aiming for a career in IT, software, or cybersecurity, this course
offers the foundation to understand how each piece fits into the broader
system.

Each chapter will be unlocked in the Library one week prior to class.

---

## **Week 1 — Roles & Responsibilities in Technology**

**Focus:** The people, teams, and structures that make the tech world run.

**Learning Goals:**  

* Identify major technical roles (CTO, sysadmin, developer, security analyst, etc.) and their responsibilities.
* Understand how cross-functional teams collaborate and depend on one another.
* Recognize how alignment across roles drives successful technology organizations.

**Activities:**

* Lecture on tech org-chart and interdependencies.
* Class discussion: “Who does what?” in real-world scenarios.
* Assignment: Complete the self-assessment and weekly questionnaire.

---

## **Week 2 — Modern Computing Hardware**

**Focus:** The physical and logical components of a computer system.

**Learning Goals:**  

* Explain how CPUs, memory (RAM), and storage interact.
* Understand I/O devices and how data moves between components.
* Differentiate between traditional and modern architectures (desktop, mobile, and cloud).

**Activities:**

* Guided breakdown of a computer teardown video or virtual hardware simulator.
* Exercise: Trace the path of a single operation from input to output.
* Assignment: Complete the Modern Computing lab, self-assessment and weekly questionnaire.

---

## **Week 3 — Computer Networking Fundamentals**

**Focus:** How data moves across networks and connects the digital world.

**Learning Goals:**

* Describe how local networks (LAN, Wi-Fi) connect to the internet.
* Understand the purpose of IP addresses, DNS, and DHCP.
* Identify the role of routers, switches, and firewalls.

**Activities:**

* Interactive demo: Tracing network hops using `ping` and `traceroute`.
* Group exercise: Map out how your home or office network connects to the web.
* Assignment: Complete the Computer Networking lab, self-assessment and weekly questionnaire.

---

## **Week 4 — Cybersecurity Fundamentals**

**Focus:** The principles of protecting people, devices, and data.

**Learning Goals:**

* Recognize common threats (phishing, malware, identity theft).
* Implement strong authentication practices (password management, MFA/2FA).
* Understand the concept of a digital fingerprint and basic privacy hygiene.

**Activities:**

* Live demo: password cracking or phishing simulation.
* Class discussion: “What makes good security habits stick?”
* Assignment: Complete the Cybersecurity lab, self-assessment and weekly questionnaire.

---

## **Week 5 — Cloud Computing**

**Focus:** How computing has shifted from personal devices to global infrastructure.

**Learning Goals:**

* Define SaaS, PaaS, and IaaS, and identify examples of each.
* Understand how AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure deliver these services.
* Recognize how virtualization and scalability power modern applications.

**Activities:**

* Intro to AWS Management Console (or simulated environment).
* Compare traditional vs. cloud deployment models.
* Assignment: Complete the Cloud Computing lab, self-assessment and weekly questionnaire.
