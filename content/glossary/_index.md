## Roles & Responsibilities
-   **Back-End Developer**  A developer who focuses on the **server-side** of an application, managing core **business logic, APIs, and databases**.  Responsible for data storage, security protocols, and performance under load. 
-   **CI/CD Pipelines** : A set of automated processes that enable developers to reliably and frequently deliver changes to the application in production. CI/CD stands for Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery (or Deployment).  
-   **Chief Information Officer (CIO)** The **internal-facing** executive who oversees all **information systems**, IT infrastructure, and technology operations that support the internal staff and business processes. Responsible for **uptime**, efficiency, and managing large internal systems and vendors.  
-   **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** The executive responsible for leading the organization's **security strategy**, risk management, and the protection of all digital assets and information. Focuses on regulatory compliance and the **CIA Triad**; reports risk posture to the CEO/Board.  
-   **Chief Technology Officer (CTO)** The **external-facing** executive who sets the organization's long-term **technology vision and strategy** regarding products and innovation. Focused on market competitiveness, R&D, and future-proofing the core product technology.  
-   **Continuous Delivery (CD)** : The practice of keeping the application's code base in a state where it is always ready to be deployed to production, often following successful CI testing.  
-   **Continuous Integration (CI)** : The practice of automatically testing and validating code changes (builds) frequently throughout the day. The goal is to catch integration errors early.  
-   **Disaster Recovery (DR)** A set of policies and procedures put in place to enable the recovery or continuation of vital technology infrastructure and systems following a major natural or human-induced disaster. A critical responsibility of the **CIO** to ensure business continuity.  
-   **Engineering Manager (EM)**  A leadership role focused on **people management, career development, and project delivery** for a small, specific team of developers (e.g., 5-10 people).  They translate the VPE's strategic goals into actionable tasks and ensure team well-being and productivity. 
-   **Front-End Developer**  A developer who focuses on the **client-side** (browser/device) of an application, building the user interface, interaction logic, and ensuring web **accessibility**.  Works with HTML, CSS, JavaScript frameworks (React, Vue, etc.). 
-   **Full-Stack Developer**  A versatile developer who works competently across all layers of the application, from the **database to the user interface**.  Highly valuable in small teams where an end-to-end feature owner is needed. 
-   **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation) A strict European Union (EU) regulation concerning data protection and privacy for all individuals within the EU and the European Economic Area. A mandatory **Regulatory Compliance** focus for any company handling EU customer data.  
-   **HIPAA** (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) A U.S. law that sets standards for protecting sensitive patient health information from being disclosed without the patient's consent or knowledge. Mandatory compliance for all healthcare and health insurance organizations.  
-   **Help Desk**  The central point of contact for internal employees seeking **technical assistance** for IT-related problems, often handling initial troubleshooting (Tier 1 support).  A key function managed under the **CIO's** Operational Oversight. 
-   **Incident Management** : The defined set of processes, roles, and tools used to respond to, resolve, and learn from unexpected service interruptions or degradation (outages).  
-   **Infrastructure as Code (IaC)** : The practice of managing and provisioning computing infrastructure (like servers, networks, and databases) through machine-readable definition files (code), rather than manual hardware configuration.  
-   **Mean Time To Recovery (MTTR)** : A key performance metric in operations that measures the average time it takes for a system or product to be restored to normal operation after an outage or incident occurs. The goal is to reduce this time as much as possible.  
-   **Monitoring & Alerting** : The system used to observe the health and performance of an application in production (Monitoring) and automatically notify the appropriate teams when specific thresholds or errors are reached (Alerting).  
-   **Operational Oversight** The responsibility for managing and maintaining the day-to-day functions of internal IT services, including help desks, network operations, and data centers. A key function of the **CIO** to ensure internal systems run smoothly.  
-   **PCI DSS** (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) A set of security standards designed to ensure that all companies that process, store, or transmit credit card information maintain a secure environment. Mandatory for any company handling customer payment data.  
-   **Project Manager (PM)**  A role responsible for overseeing the planning, execution, and closing of a specific project, managing the **Triple Constraint** (Scope, Schedule, and Budget).  Works closely with Engineering Managers to ensure technical teams deliver on time and within budget. 
-   **R&D Leadership** The executive function of directing and managing the research and development efforts aimed at creating new knowledge, products, or services. A key driver of **innovation** under the **CTO's** mandate.  
-   **Regulatory Compliance** Adhering to **laws, regulations, guidelines, and specifications relevant to the organization's business**, especially regarding **data privacy** and security. A crucial responsibility for the **CISO** and **CIO**, covering standards like **GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI DSS**.  
-   **System Uptime** The period during which a system or application is fully operational and available for use, typically measured as a percentage (e.g., 99.999% uptime). A critical **Key Metric** for the **CIO**, directly related to the **Availability** component of the CIA Triad.  
-   **Technical Debt** A metaphor representing the implied cost of additional rework caused by choosing an easy, short-term solution now instead of using a better but slower approach. A key metric for the **CTO** and VPE, aiming for its reduction to maintain product quality and scalability.  
-   **Technology Roadmapping** A **strategic planning tool** used to visually represent a technology product's major milestones, features, and required technical investments over a multi-year timeframe (e.g., 3-5 years). A core responsibility of the **CTO** to align future technical needs with business goals.  
-   **Terraform / Ansible / Jenkins / GitLab CI / Prometheus / Grafana** : Examples of common **DevOps Tools** used to implement **IaC**, automation, monitoring, and **CI/CD**.  
-   **The CIA Triad** A foundational model for security policy; its components are **Confidentiality** (preventing unauthorized access), **Integrity** (ensuring data accuracy), and **Availability** (ensuring systems are accessible). The central focus of the **CISO's** entire security strategy.  
-   **Vendor Management** The process of selecting, managing, and maintaining business relationships with third-party software, hardware, and service providers (e.g., cloud platforms, ERP systems). Handled by the **CIO's** office to control costs and service quality.  
-   **Vice President of Engineering (VPE)**  The senior leader who manages the entire engineering department, reporting to the CTO. Responsible for departmental strategy, **hiring/retention**, and overall **execution** of the technical roadmap.  Manages the Engineering Managers and focuses on scaling the entire workforce. 

## Modern Computing
-   **AI (Artificial Intelligence)**: The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems, including learning, reasoning, and self-correction.  
-   **API (Application Programming Interface)**  A set of rules and protocols that allows different software applications to communicate with each other, often used by the Front-End to request data from the Back-End.  The standard method for communication between services, core to **Back-End** development. 
-   **Android**: A mobile operating system developed by Google, based on the Linux kernel and designed primarily for touchscreen mobile devices such as smartphones and tablets.    
-   **App Store**: A digital distribution platform for mobile applications, often specific to a particular mobile operating system (e.g., Apple's App Store, Google Play Store).  
-   **CPU (Central Processing Unit)**: The "brain" of a computer, responsible for executing instructions and processing data.  
-   **Client-Side**  Operations performed by the application within the user's **web browser or mobile device**.  The domain of the **Front-End Developer**. 
-   **Cloud**: A network of remote servers hosted on the internet and used to store, manage, and process data instead of a local server or a personal computer.  
-   **IoT (Internet of Things)**: A network of physical objects ("things") embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies to connect and exchange data with other devices and systems over the internet.  
-   **Machine Learning**: A subfield of AI that gives computers the ability to learn without being explicitly programmed, often using statistical techniques to find patterns in data.  
-   **Operating System (OS)**: The core software that manages all the hardware and software resources of a computer system and provides a platform for applications to run on.  
-   **RAM (Random Access Memory)**: A type of **temporary, volatile memory** used to store data that the CPU needs to access quickly.  
-   **Server-Side**  Operations performed by the application on a **remote server** (or cloud), including processing requests, running business logic, and managing data access.  The domain of the **Back-End Developer**. 
-   **Storage**: The **non-volatile** component of a computer that stores data for long-term use (e.g., SSDs and HDDs).  
-   **Touchscreen**: An input device that allows users to interact with a display by using their fingers or a stylus.   
-   **Virtual Machine (VM)**  An emulation of a computer system that provides the functionality of a physical computer, running on another server.  A fundamental component of **Cloud** and **IaaS** offerings. 
-   **iOS**: A **mobile operating system** created and developed by Apple Inc. exclusively for its hardware, including the **iPhone** and **iPad**.   

## Computer Networking
-   **Ethernet**: A family of **wired** computer networking technologies commonly used to connect devices in local area networks (LANs).  
-   **Firewall**: A network security device that monitors and filters incoming and outgoing network traffic based on an organization's previously established security policies.  
-   **IP Address**: A unique numerical label assigned to each device connected to a computer network that uses the Internet Protocol for communication.  
-   **Local Area Network (LAN)**  A computer network that interconnects devices within a limited area, such as a single home, school, or office building.  The type of network most commonly managed by internal IT teams under the **CIO**. 
-   **Router**: A device that forwards data packets between computer networks, acting as the **traffic manager of a network**.  
-   **Switch**  A networking device that connects multiple devices within the same network segment (a LAN) and forwards data only to the intended recipient device.  Works at a lower level than a **Router**, managing traffic *within* a local network. 
-   **VPN (Virtual Private Network)**: A technology that creates a **secure and encrypted connection** over a less secure network, such as the internet.  
-   **Wi-Fi**: A **wireless networking** technology that allows devices to connect to the internet or communicate with one another without physical cables.  

## Cybersecurity
-   **Encryption**  The process of converting information or data into a code (ciphertext) to prevent unauthorized access. It is reversed by decryption.  Essential for ensuring **Confidentiality** (part of the CIA Triad) and meeting compliance standards (e.g., **GDPR**). 
-   **Malware**: Short for "malicious software," it's any software intentionally designed to cause damage to a computer, server, or network.  
-   **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**  A security system that requires users to provide **two or more verification factors** to gain access to a resource, greatly reducing the risk of unauthorized access.  A key security policy enforced by the **CISO** and IT teams. 
-   **Phishing**: A type of **cyber attack** in which an attacker tricks a user into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card numbers, by impersonating a trustworthy entity.  

## Cloud Computing
-   **IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service)**: A type of cloud computing that provides virtualized computing resources over the internet, such as virtual machines, storage, and networking (e.g., Amazon Web Services).  
-   **PaaS (Platform as a Service)**: A cloud computing model that provides a platform and environment to allow developers to build, run, and manage applications without the complexity of building and maintaining the infrastructure (e.g., Heroku).  
-   **SaaS (Software as a Service)**: A software distribution model in which a third-party provider hosts applications and makes them available to customers over the internet (e.g., Google Docs, Microsoft 365).  
-   **Serverless Computing**  A cloud model where the cloud provider manages the server infrastructure, automatically scaling resources, allowing developers to focus purely on code (functions) without managing underlying operating systems.  A specific technical implementation often used by **DevOps/Cloud Engineers** to increase efficiency and reduce operational overhead. 
-   **Virtual Private Cloud (VPC)**  A private, isolated section of a public cloud (like AWS or Azure) where an organization can launch resources, providing a secure boundary for their services.  Used by **Cloud Engineers** to create secure network environments within the public cloud.
